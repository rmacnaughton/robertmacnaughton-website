# Coaching Website Strategy

## Core Philosophy

This website is **not** a marketing tool. It serves three specific purposes:
1. Be findable when someone searches your name after a referral
2. Reassure prospects who are already in conversation with you
3. Support the decision when shared with a spouse, boss, or colleague

90% of visits happen *after* a sales call — not before. The website validates, it does not sell.

---

## What This Website Is NOT For

- Catching random passersby or converting casual browsers
- Building email lists or capturing leads
- Convincing skeptics to become clients
- Establishing credibility from scratch (that comes from referrals and personal conversations)

Coaching requires higher trust than product sales. A website alone cannot establish that trust — and trying to do so will undermine credibility.

---

## What to Avoid

### Copy & Messaging
- Generic buzzword language: "uplevel," "transform," "unleash unlimited power"
- Vague benefit statements that don't resonate with serious buyers
- "Who it's for" sections or benefit lists
- Describing problems the prospect doesn't have
- Over-niching that excludes good prospects

### Tactics to Skip Entirely
- Lead magnets, free giveaways, honey pots
- CRM integrations or mailing list sign-ups
- Urgency or scarcity messaging
- Sales videos
- Internet marketing conversion tactics of any kind

### Impulse-buyer psychology
- Good coaching clients are high-commitment, considered decision makers
- Do not target or appeal to impulse buyers

---

## What to Include

### Purpose 1: Be Findable
- **Name in the domain URL**
- **Name in the largest text, upper-left of the page**
- Get links from other sites with your name visible
- Create 5–10 videos on others' channels with your name in the title

### Purpose 2: Reassure Prospects
- **Specific, impressive statistics** — not vague promises
  - e.g. "30,000 paid coaching sessions completed"
  - e.g. "$1.2B raised by clients in 24 months"
  - e.g. "70+ months average client retention"
- **High-status testimonials** with real names, titles, and companies
  - Focus on CEO titles, unicorn companies, recognizable backgrounds
  - Results-focused, not complimentary
  - No generic praise without attribution
- **Clear, direct messaging** for the target demographic

### Purpose 3: Support the Sharing Decision
- When a prospect shares the site with a spouse, boss, or colleague, it should pass scrutiny
- Stats and credentials help third parties validate the investment
- Professional polish: no broken links, no placeholder content, no outdated dates

---

## Technical Requirements

- Modern, maintained appearance
- No broken links or placeholder sections
- No dead blogs or empty galleries
- No past dates framed as upcoming events
- Easy, prominent contact method
- Professional but not over-produced

---

## Optional Elements (Use With Caution)

### Proxy Coaching Experience (Video/Audio)
- Only include if exceptionally well-produced and genuinely insightful
- Most coaching clips *unsell* prospects — omit if uncertain
- Be mindful of client privacy with session recordings

### Blog
- Better to have **no blog** than an abandoned one
- If included, posts must be recent and actively maintained

---

## Quick Reference Rules for Claude Code

When building or editing any part of this site, apply these rules:

- [ ] Name appears in domain and largest text, upper-left
- [ ] No lead capture forms, newsletter sign-ups, or CRM hooks
- [ ] No urgency, scarcity, or conversion copywriting patterns
- [ ] All statistics are specific and impressive (not vague)
- [ ] All testimonials include real name, title, and company
- [ ] Contact method is prominent and functional
- [ ] No blog section unless content is ready and current
- [ ] No placeholder text, broken links, or outdated dates
- [ ] Copy avoids buzzwords and vague transformation language
